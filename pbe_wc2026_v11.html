① PBE — Predictive Bayesian Ensemble (v11: EQV-enhanced)

PBE = (1−ew)×Prior + ew×Likelihood + StageBonus Prior (v11) = (1−β)×HistPrior + β×normEV [β = 0.10] HistPrior = ConfStr × HistPrestige + CoachBonus normEV = (EQV_team − EQV_min) / (EQV_max − EQV_min) → [0,1] EQV = Sponsorship brand equity value (USD M, EMW Global 2026) Likelihood = 0.30×Goals + 0.25×Defense + 0.20×Poss + 0.25×ShotEfficiency StageBonus = +0.08 (≥7pts) · +0.04 (4–6pts) · +0.01 (1–3pts) ew = min(0.35 + matches×0.10, 0.65) [evidence weight grows]

v11 change — EQV integration (Approach 2, Additive, β=0.10): The prior now blends 90% historical record with 10% normalised sponsorship brand equity value. EQV (EMW Global Athlete Brand Economy Report, SportBusiness April 2026) measures media equivalency value — converting social reach and engagement into equivalent paid-media cost. It is distinct from Transfermarkt squad values: EQV captures current commercial relevance, which correlates with squad depth and elite-club exposure that historical prestige underweights.

The integration was validated via sandbox simulation across 12 confirmed WC 2026 group-stage results. Correlation analysis: PBE Prior ↔ EQV: r=0.828 (R²=68.6%), leaving 31.4% independent variance — genuine new signal. Accuracy improvement: 63%→75% on non-draw matches (5/8→6/8). Key case: USA vs Paraguay (actual 4-1) — EQV gap ($310M vs $55M) correctly flipped the predicted favourite from Paraguay to USA.

β=0.10 (conservative) per conditional adoption recommendation. Validate at R16+ (n≥50 non-draw matches) before raising to β=0.15. EQV is a structural input, frozen pre-tournament — it does not update from match results (analogous to C5S). Original analysis: Fredy Salazar, PBE Economic Integration Report, June 2026.

② ELO — FIFA-style Elo Rating

E = 1 / (1 + 10^((Rb−Ra)/400))
Ra' = Ra + K × (S − E)
K = 60 (WC), goal_weight = 1 + ln(1 + |GD|)

Classic chess-derived rating adapted for football. K=60 for World Cup matches. Goal difference inflates K via logarithmic multiplier. Win probability derived from rating difference.

③ POI — Independent Poisson Goal Model

λH = avgGoals × AttH × DefA
λA = avgGoals × AttA × DefH
avgGoals = 2.74 (hydration-calibrated)
P(score) = e^(−λ) × λ^k / k!
Simulate 0–6 goals each team

Each team's expected goals modelled as independent Poisson processes. Attack/defense coefficients derived from pre-tournament historical scoring rates. v7 calibration: avgGoals raised from 2.60 → 2.74 (+5.4%) to account for WC 2026 mandatory 3-min hydration breaks (×2 per match): fresher players in 2nd half, net ~4.5 min extra stoppage time, and Club World Cup 2025 format signal (same break structure, avg 2.84 goals/game). Home advantage multiplier removed (neutral venue). Draw floor implicit in λ symmetry.

④ MKT — Market-Implied Probability

P_mkt(W) = BaseOdds × FormMod × RankMod
FormMod = 0.5 + 0.5×(recentW/recentM)
RankMod = 1 − 0.3×(FIFARank_gap/100)

Replicates bookmaker consensus using FIFA ranking differentials and recent form (last 5 qualifying matches). Normalised to sum to 1 after margin removal. v7 calibration: home advantage multiplier removed (neutral venue, PR1 finding confirmed by 10-match simulation). Draw floor raised from 0.12 → 0.135 (+1.5pp) to reflect WC 2026 hydration break tactical reset effect. Goal rate updated to 2.74.

★ ENS — Integrated Ensemble (6 models)

ENS = 0.25×PBE + 0.22×ELO + 0.20×POI + 0.15×MKT + 0.18×C5S

Six-model weighted combination. v11: PBE prior now includes EQV brand equity (β=0.10), so the ensemble implicitly carries economic value through the PBE channel. All 5 base models update dynamically after each result (C5S excepted). Predictions for each match use model state trained on all prior results only — no lookahead. PBE leads (Bayesian evidence + EQV). ELO second. POI third. C5S fourth (structural, frozen). MKT fifth (rolling form).

⚠️ Illustrative heuristic. v11: EQV integrated into PBE prior at β=0.10 (conditional adoption per June 2026 analytical report). All v7 hydration calibrations and v9 sequential replay architecture preserved.

⑤ C5S — Five Capitals Score

C5S = 0.28×HistCap + 0.22×EconCap + 0.18×HumCap + 0.17×SportCap + 0.15×DevTraj H: WC wins, participations, win rate E: GDP size, GDP per capita, growth, stability Hm: HDI, political stability, diaspora S: Sport investment, conf. strength, host status T: P2 vs P1 improvement (momentum) Pairwise: P(H) = C5S_H / (C5S_H + C5S_A) · draw floor = 0.18

Structural capital model based on the Multidimensional Sport Human Capital (MSHC) framework, designed by Fredy Salazar. All five dimensions min-max scaled to [0,1] across all 48 nations. C5S is frozen for the entire tournament — structural capitals (economy, HDI, sport institutions) are slow-moving by nature and do not respond to individual match outcomes. This orthogonality to PBE/ELO/POI/MKT is precisely its value: it captures what those models cannot see. Pairwise ratio prediction with draw floor 0.18 (slightly higher than PBE/MKT because structural models lack fatigue and momentum signal).

C5S is set as a default filter alongside PBE — together they show the structural pre-tournament view (C5S) and the evolving in-tournament Bayesian view (PBE), which is the most informative pair for tracking how match reality aligns with structural expectations. Key rankings: Brazil leads (1.000, historical capital), Germany 2nd (0.928), USA 3rd (0.657, economic capital). Biggest C5S vs ELO divergence: Qatar (C5S rank 7, ELO rank ~30) — structural investment without on-pitch pedigree.
